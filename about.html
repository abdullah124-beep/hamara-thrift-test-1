How It All Started

It started with a simple problem: too many great sneakers being thrown away, and too many people who couldn't afford premium brands new.

Abdullah Arshad — a sneakerhead from Wapda Town, Multan — had been collecting and trading shoes as a hobby for years. He noticed that friends and family were getting far better value from his carefully maintained pairs than anything available at retail.

So he made it official. Hamara Thrift was born with a simple mission: make premium sneaker culture accessible to everyone in Pakistan.

What started as an Instagram page run from his home turned into a full online operation — sourcing, cleaning, grading, photographing, and listing every pair with complete honesty. The response from across Pakistan has been incredible.

Today, Hamara Thrift has sold over 500 pairs, built a loyal community of sneaker lovers across the city, and proved that second-hand doesn't mean second-best.

Sustainability

Every pair saved is a
win for the planet

The global fashion industry is one of the largest polluters on Earth. Millions of pairs of shoes are discarded every year — many of them perfectly wearable, just no longer wanted.

At Hamara Thrift, we fight back against that waste by extending the life of every pair we sell. When you buy from us, you're not just getting a great deal — you're making a real, measurable environmental choice.

🌱
Lower Carbon
Producing a new shoe emits ~14kg CO₂. Buying second-hand avoids that entirely.
♻️
Circular Economy
We keep shoes in circulation, reducing landfill and the demand for new production.

His Story

"I just wanted everyone in Pakistan to have access to premium sneakers without breaking the bank."

Abdullah grew up in Multan with a passion for sneakers that his budget couldn't always support. He discovered that with the right care, premium shoes could last for years beyond their first owner — and that resale wasn't just practical, it was ethical.

After years of trading shoes informally, he launched Hamara Thrift in 2022, building every process from scratch — from sourcing authentic pairs to developing his own condition grading system that customers could actually trust.

His philosophy is simple: "If I wouldn't wear it, I won't sell it." That personal standard has become the backbone of Hamara Thrift's reputation across Pakistan.
